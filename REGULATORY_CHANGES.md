# BANGALORE RENTAL MARKET REGULATORY CHANGES - 2024-2025

## Executive Summary

The Karnataka government has introduced significant changes to rental regulations that directly impact landlords and tenants in Bangalore. These changes aim to formalize the rental market, protect tenant rights, and provide clearer frameworks for dispute resolution.

---

## Key Regulatory Changes

### 1. Model Tenancy Act (MTA) Implementation

**Status:** Karnataka has adopted framework based on the Model Tenancy Act 2021

**Key Provisions:**
- **Security Deposit Cap:** Maximum 2 months' rent for residential properties (previously was often 10+ months in Bangalore)
- **Rent Escalation:** Annual rent increase capped at specific rates (typically inflation-linked)
- **Written Agreements:** Mandatory written tenancy agreements for all rentals
- **Registration:** Rental agreements must be registered
- **Digital Documentation:** Acceptance of digital signatures and electronic records

**Impact:**
- **Landlords:** Must formalize agreements, can't demand excessive deposits
- **Tenants:** Better legal protection, capped deposit requirements

---

### 2. Karnataka Rent Control Act Updates

**Key Changes:**
- **Rent Tribunal:** Establishment of Rent Authority and Rent Tribunals for dispute resolution
- **Time Limits:** Strict timelines for eviction processes
- **Tenant Rights:** Enhanced protections against arbitrary eviction
- **Maintenance Obligations:** Clearer definitions of landlord vs tenant maintenance responsibilities

---

### 3. Digital Police Verification System

**Implementation:** Online tenant police verification system
- **Karnataka One Portal:** Online submission of tenant verification
- **Digital Tracking:** Track verification status online
- **Timelines:** 30-day verification process
- **Integration:** Linked with rental agreement registration

---

### 4. Stamp Duty and Registration Fee Changes

**e-Stamping:**
- Mandatory e-stamping for rental agreements above certain values
- Digital payment options
- Reduced processing times

**Registration Requirements:**
- Agreements above 11 months must be registered
- Digital registration options available
- Integration with CERSAI (Central Registry)

---

### 5. Digital Agreement Platforms

**Legal Recognition:**
- E-stamped agreements legally recognized
- Aadhaar-based eSign valid
- WhatsApp communication admissible in disputes
- Email exchanges as evidence
