# KARNATAKA RENTAL LAW CHANGES - JANUARY 2026
## Karnataka Rent (Amendment) Bill, 2025 - Full Implementation

### Executive Summary

The Karnataka Rent (Amendment) Bill, 2025 has fully come into effect as of January 2026, bringing the state's rental laws into alignment with the central government's Model Tenancy Act (MTA). This represents the most significant overhaul of Bangalore's rental market in decades, ending the era of informal agreements and massive security deposits.

---

## Key Legal Changes (Effective Jan 2026)

### 1. Security Deposit Cap - The End of 10-Month Deposits

**Previous Standard:** 10 months' rent (Bangalore's infamous norm)

**New Law:**
- **Residential Properties:** Maximum 2 months' rent
- **Commercial Properties:** Maximum 6 months' rent

**Impact:**
- **Tenants:** Massive financial relief - easier entry for professionals, migrants, students
- **Landlords:** Loss of liquidity buffer previously used for EMIs, investments

---

### 2. Mandatory Written & Registered Agreements

**New Requirements:**
- **Digital Registration:** All agreements must be registered via Kaveri 2.0 portal
- **Timeline:** Within 60 days of execution
- **Authority:** Jointly submitted to newly established "Rent Authority"
- **Oral Agreements:** No longer legally enforceable

**Compliance:**
- Written agreements only
- Digital signatures valid
- Aadhaar eSign accepted
- WhatsApp/electronic communication admissible as evidence

---

### 3. Regulated Rent Increases

**New Rules:**
- **Frequency:** Rent can only be increased once per year
- **Cap:** Pre-defined in agreement (typically 5-10%)
- **Notice Period:** 90-day written notice required before any increase
- **Documentation:** Must be documented and registered

---

### 4. Decriminalization & New Penalty Structure

**Major Change:**
- **Previous:** Certain violations could lead to imprisonment
- **Now:** Civil penalties only

**Penalty Structure:**
- Minor violations: Fines up to ₹5,000
- Serious violations: Double the rent amount
- No criminal record implications

---

### 5. Fast-Track Dispute Resolution - Rent Courts & Tribunals

**New System:**
- **Rent Courts:** Replace slow civil courts for rental disputes
- **Timeline:** Cases resolved within 60 days (vs. years in civil court)
- **Jurisdiction:** All rental disputes, eviction cases, deposit disputes
- **Accessibility:** Simplified procedures, lower costs

**Landlord Benefits:**
- Faster evictions for non-paying tenants
- Quicker resolution of overstay situations
- Double rent penalty for overstay after lease expiry

**Tenant Benefits:**
- Swift resolution of deposit disputes
- Protection against harassment
- Faster relief for essential service cutoffs

---

### 6. Clear Maintenance & Wear-and-Tear Rules

**Landlord Responsibility:**
- Structural repairs
- Major plumbing/electrical issues
- **CANNOT deduct for:** Normal wear and tear (fading paint, minor scuffs)

**Tenant Responsibility:**
- Day-to-day maintenance (leaky taps, minor fixes)
- Damage caused by tenant (broken fixtures, wall holes)

**Documentation Required:**
- Move-in/move-out photos with timestamps
- Written estimates for repairs
- Itemized deduction lists

---

### 7. Right to Privacy - 24-Hour Notice Rule

**New Tenant Protection:**
- **Notice Required:** Minimum 24-hour written/electronic notice
- **Method:** Email, WhatsApp, SMS accepted
- **Exceptions:** Emergency situations only
- **Penalty:** Violation can result in fines and legal action

**Landlord Obligations:**
- Cannot enter unannounced
- Must respect tenant's right to peaceful possession
- Must schedule inspections at reasonable hours

---

### 8. Protection of Essential Services

**Critical Tenant Protection:**
- **Prohibited:** Landlords cannot cut off water, electricity, gas during disputes
- **Violation:** Immediate legal recourse available
- **Penalty:** Heavy fines for service interruption
- **Remedy:** Tenant can seek emergency court orders

**This protects tenants from:**
- Utility blackmail during deposit disputes
- Forced evictions via service cuts
- Harassment tactics

---

### 9. Overstay Penalties

**New Rule:**
- **Penalty:** Double rent for every month of overstay after lease expiry
- **Enforcement:** Fast-track through Rent Tribunals
- **No Grace Period:** Penalty applies from day 1 of overstay

## Current Market Reality (March 2026)

### Transition Phase Challenges

**Enforcement Gap:**
- Many landlords/brokers in high-demand areas (Indiranagar, Whitefield) still demanding 5-6 month deposits
- Resistance to formal registration process
- Awareness still spreading among tenants

**Shifting Slowly:**
- Digital infrastructure (Kaveri 2.0) becoming mandatory for utility transfers
- Professional property managers adopting compliance
- Educated tenants increasingly asserting rights
